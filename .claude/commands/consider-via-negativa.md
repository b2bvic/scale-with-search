# Via Negativa (Improvement by Subtraction)

## When to Use

Invoke when feeling overextended, when adding more isn't working, when complexity has accumulated without justification, when a system or schedule is bloated, or when the instinct is to add a new tool/process/habit but the real problem is carrying too much. Nassim Taleb's principle: we gain more by removing the wrong things than by adding the right things.

## Execution

1. **Inventory everything.** List all commitments, activities, tools, processes, subscriptions, habits, obligations, and recurring tasks. Include the ones that feel mandatory — those are the most important to examine.

2. **For each item, ask: "What happens if I stop?"** Three possible answers:
   - **Nothing meaningful.** This is dead weight. Cut it.
   - **Short-term disruption, long-term irrelevance.** The withdrawal pain is real but temporary. Likely worth cutting.
   - **Genuine damage.** This is load-bearing. Keep it.

3. **Identify the silent costs.** Items that seem harmless often carry hidden costs:
   - Cognitive overhead (remembering, tracking, context-switching)
   - Maintenance burden (updates, renewals, upkeep)
   - Opportunity cost (time spent here can't be spent elsewhere)
   - Complexity tax (each addition makes the whole system harder to reason about)

4. **Rank by removal value.** Which items, if subtracted, would yield the most relief, clarity, or freed capacity? The best cuts are high-cost items with low-value output.

5. **Propose the subtraction list.** Name specific things to stop, cancel, unsubscribe from, decline, or abandon. Be concrete — "reduce meetings" is vague. "Decline the Thursday sync and cancel the Monday standup" is actionable.

6. **Protect what remains.** After subtraction, what's left should be the essentials — the activities that justify their cost. These deserve more time and attention, not less. Subtraction creates space for what matters.

### The Litmus Test

If you can't articulate why something is on your plate in one sentence, it probably shouldn't be.

## Output Format

```
## Via Negativa: [Context]

### Full Inventory
[List of all commitments/activities/tools under review]

### Subtraction Analysis
| Item | What Happens If Stopped | Silent Costs | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Item] | [Impact of removal] | [Hidden costs of keeping] | Cut / Keep / Reduce |

### Recommended Cuts
1. [Item]: [How to stop and what it frees up]
2. ...

### What Remains (and deserves more)
- [Essential item]: [Why it stays and how freed capacity strengthens it]

### Net Effect
[What the world looks like after subtraction — time recovered, complexity reduced, focus sharpened]
```
