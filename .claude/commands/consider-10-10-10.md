# 10/10/10 Time Horizon Analysis

## When to Use

Invoke when torn between short-term comfort and long-term benefit, when emotions are driving a decision, when procrastination disguises itself as prudence, or when you need to separate what feels urgent from what actually matters across time.

## Execution

1. **State the decision.** Frame it as a binary or small set of options. "Should I X or Y?" works better than vague deliberation.

2. **Evaluate at 10 minutes.** How will you feel about this decision in 10 minutes? What is the immediate emotional response? This captures:
   - Relief or anxiety
   - Social pressure or approval
   - Comfort or discomfort
   - Impulse satisfaction or regret

   The 10-minute lens reveals what your emotions want.

3. **Evaluate at 10 months.** How will this decision look in 10 months? What has changed? This captures:
   - Whether short-term pain converts to medium-term gain
   - Habit formation or erosion
   - Relationship impacts that compound
   - Financial or career trajectory shifts
   - Whether you'll remember the 10-minute discomfort at all

   The 10-month lens reveals what your rational self wants.

4. **Evaluate at 10 years.** How will this decision look in 10 years? This captures:
   - Identity-level consequences (who you become)
   - Compounding effects (skills, relationships, wealth, health)
   - Regret potential — "I wish I had..." vs "I'm glad I did..."
   - Whether this decision matters at all at that timescale

   The 10-year lens reveals what your future self wants.

5. **Identify the conflict.** Where do the three horizons disagree? Typically, the 10-minute answer pulls one direction while 10-month and 10-year pull another. Name the tension.

6. **Recommend based on the longest relevant horizon.** Default to the 10-year perspective unless the decision is genuinely irrelevant at that timescale (in which case, go with 10 months). The 10-minute perspective should almost never win.

## Output Format

```
## 10/10/10 Analysis: [Decision]

### The Decision
[Options being weighed]

### 10 Minutes
[Immediate emotional impact and reaction]

### 10 Months
[Medium-term practical consequences]

### 10 Years
[Long-term identity and compounding effects]

### The Conflict
[Where the horizons disagree and why]

### Recommendation
[Which option wins when viewed from the longest relevant horizon — and why]
```
